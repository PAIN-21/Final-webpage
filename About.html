Introduction

Ah, the Big Three in the world of anime - they're like the three musketeers of action-packed, adrenaline-pumping, and downright awesome shows! These powerhouse series have captured the hearts and minds of anime fans all over the world, and they're basically the holy trinity of epicness.

The nickname "The Big Three" came about due to their immense worldwide popularity and length. With that, they were always featured in front of the shared Shonen Jump covers and always had bigger images for their main protagonists: Luffy, Naruto and Ichigo. All three series ran for at least 15 years and 70 volumes, and all three overlapped in publication from 2001-2014

Bleach

The "Big Three" refers to three iconic and immensely popular shonen anime series that dominated the anime scene during the mid-2000s to early 2010s. These series include "Naruto," "Bleach," and "One Piece." Each of these shows boasts a unique storyline, memorable characters, and intense battles, making them beloved by fans worldwide. The Big Three played a significant role in introducing countless viewers to the world of anime and have left an enduring mark on the history.

"Bleach" is a popular Japanese anime and manga series created by Tite Kubo. It follows the story of Ichigo Kurosaki, a teenager who gains the powers of a Soul Reaper after meeting Rukia Kuchiki, another Soul Reaper. With these new abilities, Ichigo must protect humans from evil spirits called Hollows and guide the souls of the deceased to the afterlife. The series is known for its action-packed battles, unique characters, and intricate storyline. "Bleach" has gained a large fanbase worldwide and is considered one of the iconic series in the anime community.

"Naruto" is a well-known Japanese anime and manga series created by Masashi Kishimoto. It centers around Naruto Uzumaki, a young ninja with dreams of becoming the strongest ninja and the leader of his village, known as the Hokage.

One Piece

One Piece follows Monkey D. Luffy and his pirate crew as they search for the legendary One Piece treasure in order to become the Pirate King.

"One Piece" is a famous Japanese anime and manga series created by Eiichiro Oda. The story follows Monkey D. Luffy, a spirited young pirate with the ability to stretch his body like rubber after eating a magical fruit. Luffy dreams of becoming the Pirate King by finding the legendary treasure known as "One Piece."
